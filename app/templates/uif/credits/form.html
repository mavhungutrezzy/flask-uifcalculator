How Are UIF Credits Calculated?

UIF credits are calculated using the current UIF credit system where:

  • 1 credit day is earned for every 4 days worked
  • Maximum benefit duration is 365 days over a 4-year period

This means employees who contributed to UIF consistently over several years may qualify for the maximum UIF benefit period.

If you worked fewer days or had contribution gaps, your UIF credits and payout duration may be lower.

How Much Does UIF Pay?

UIF payments are calculated using a sliding scale based on your salary. Lower-income earners generally receive a higher percentage replacement rate than higher-income earners.

Most employees receive between 38% and 60% of their salary, subject to UIF limits and available credits.

Your actual UIF payout depends on:

  • salary history,
  • UIF contributions,
  • and benefit type.

Frequently Asked Questions About UIF Credits

Common questions about UIF credit calculations and benefit duration in South Africa.

What is a UIF credit?

A UIF credit is a benefit day earned through UIF contributions while employed. The more days you work and contribute to UIF, the more credit days you accumulate.

How are UIF credits calculated?

You earn 1 UIF credit day for every 4 days worked. This means if you worked 240 days in a year while contributing to UIF, you would earn 60 credit days.

How much is UIF first payment?

The amount depends on your salary, credits, and contribution history. First payments typically take several weeks to process and may be lower than expected due to banking verification or incomplete employer documentation.

How much does UIF pay out?

UIF payouts vary based on salary and available credits. Most employees receive between 38% and 60% of their salary, subject to UIF salary limits and the maximum payout ceiling of approximately R65,000.

Can I calculate UIF payout online?

Yes, you can use our UIF payout calculator to estimate benefits online. The tool helps you understand your expected payout before submitting your claim.

What is the maximum UIF benefit period?

The maximum UIF benefit duration is usually 365 days over 4 years of contributions. This applies to unemployment benefits. Maternity and adoption benefits have a fixed maximum of 121 days.

How do I check my UIF credits?

You can check your UIF credits by logging into your uFiling account, visiting your nearest Labour Centre, or using the UIF USSD code on your mobile phone.

What if my UIF credits are too low?

Low UIF credits may indicate missing contributions or employer declarations. Visit your Labour Centre with your payslips to verify your contribution record. Missing UIF contributions can often be recovered with proper documentation.
