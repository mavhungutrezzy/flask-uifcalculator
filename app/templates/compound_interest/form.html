What Is Compound Interest? (The South African Context)

Compound interest is interest calculated on your initial principal plus all accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest, where you only earn on the original amount, compound interest creates a snowball effect where your money grows exponentially.

In South Africa, this distinction is crucial. With inflation often around 4-6% annually, your money needs to grow faster than inflation to maintain purchasing power.

Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)nt

  • A = Final amount in Rands
  • P = Principal (starting investment)
  • r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
  • n = Compounding frequency per year
  • t = Time in years

How to Calculate Compound Interest: 4 Methods

  1. Mental estimate: Rule of 72 (72 / rate).
  2. Exact formula: Use A = P(1 + r/n)nt.
  3. Online calculators: Use South African bank and comparison tools.
  4. Spreadsheet: `=FV(8%/12, 60, 0, -20000)` for a basic example.
