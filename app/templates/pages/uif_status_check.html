How to Check UIF Status Online

The main way to check your UIF status online is through the official uFiling website. It can show your claim progress, case number, payment status and contribution history.

  1. Visit the official uFiling website
    Go to the UIF uFiling login page.
  2. Log in or register
    Use your South African ID number and password. If you do not have an account yet, register before checking your claim.
  3. Open your claim details
    From the dashboard, choose Benefit Payments, then select View My Claims.
  4. Read your claim status
    Check the latest status, case number and payment notes. For example, Sent to Assessor usually means the claim is under review.

Check UIF Status by USSD

If you do not have internet access, you can check from a mobile phone.

  • Dial *134*843#
  • Follow the prompts for Claims and Payment Status
  • Keep your ID number and claim information nearby

What Your UIF Status Means

UIF wording can be confusing. These are common status messages claimants see while a claim is being processed or paid.

Review

Sent to Assessor

Your claim is being reviewed by UIF. This does not yet mean it has been approved or rejected.

Payment

Payment Reserved

A payment may be ready for processing, but it still needs to move through the payment system.

Action needed

Payment Blocked

Banking verification may have failed, or your details may need correction. UIF may request UI 2.8 and UI 49 forms.

Closed

Payment Finalised

Your available credits may be used up, or no further payments are due on that claim.

Important: If your status says Claim Finalised or No credit days remaining, you may have used your available UIF credits for that claim. Confirm the final decision with UIF or the Department of Employment and Labour.

Other Common UIF Status Messages

  • Cancelled: the application was stopped; contact UIF for the reason.
  • Auto Close: the application may still be waiting for processing.
  • Parked: outstanding documents may be required before the claim can continue.
  • Payment Reversed: a payment bounced, often because banking or surname details do not match.
  • Completed by Assessor: an assessor has processed the file, but the outcome may still vary.
  • Sent to Paymaster: a payment request has been sent for payment processing.
  • Cancelled by Paymaster: the specific payment request was stopped.
  • Pending Adjudicate: missing employer declarations may be affecting your credit days.
