How Long Is Maternity Leave in South Africa?

Under the Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA), every employee in South Africa is legally entitled to 4 consecutive months of maternity leave. This applies regardless of how long you have worked for your employer.

Maternity leave may start:

  • at any time from 4 weeks before your expected due date, or
  • on the day of your child's birth.

If your baby is born before your planned leave start date, maternity leave begins on the date of birth. After the birth, you must take at least 6 weeks of compulsory maternity leave before returning to work — your employer cannot ask you to return earlier.

Does Your Employer Have to Pay You During Maternity Leave?

No. South African law does not require employers to pay salaries during maternity leave. The BCEA only guarantees the right to take unpaid leave.

Your income during maternity leave comes from the UIF maternity benefit — a payment made by the Unemployment Insurance Fund, not your employer. Some employers voluntarily top up your pay to 100% of your salary, but this is not a legal requirement.

Always check your employment contract for any company maternity pay policy.

How Much Will UIF Pay During Maternity Leave?

UIF maternity benefits are calculated using a sliding income replacement rate (IRR). The lower your salary, the higher your replacement rate — up to a maximum of 60%. Higher earners receive a lower percentage, but never less than 38%.

The benefit is capped at the UIF salary ceiling of R17 712 per month (2026). If you earn more than this, your benefit is calculated as if you earn R17 712.

Who Qualifies for UIF Maternity Benefits?

To qualify for maternity UIF, you must:

  • be employed and going on official maternity leave (not resigning),
  • have contributed to UIF for at least 13 weeks before your claim,
  • be registered as a UIF contributor (your employer deducts 1% of your salary monthly),
  • be a South African citizen or permanent resident, or hold a valid work permit.
Important: If you resign before going on maternity leave, you forfeit your UIF maternity benefits. You must remain employed and take official maternity leave to qualify.

Who does not qualify?

  • Employees who resign voluntarily before leave
  • Domestic workers excluded from UIF (rare since 2003 amendments — most domestic workers are now covered)
  • Self-employed or freelance workers not registered for UIF
  • Workers employed for fewer than 24 hours per month

Documents Required for Maternity UIF

Gather these before submitting your claim to avoid delays:

  1. South African ID (original + certified copy)
  2. UI-2.7 form — maternity claim form, completed and signed by your doctor, midwife, or clinic
  3. UI-19 form — employer declaration completed by your employer
  4. UI-2.8 form — banking details form (your bank account details for payment)
  5. 3-month bank statement in your name
  6. Proof of pregnancy — sick note, hospital letter, or birth certificate (after delivery)

After the birth, you will also need a birth certificate to continue or finalise your claim.

How to Apply for Maternity UIF (Step by Step)

Option A: Apply online via uFiling

  1. Register or log in at uifonline.labour.gov.za
  2. Go to Maternity Benefit under the claims section
  3. Complete the online application and upload your documents
  4. Submit and note your claim reference number
  5. Each month, submit the UI-2.8 continuation form to keep receiving payments

Option B: Apply in person at a Labour Centre

  1. Find your nearest Department of Labour office
  2. Bring all original documents listed above
  3. Complete the application forms at the Labour Centre
  4. Collect your reference number
Apply early. Submit your maternity UIF claim at least 8 weeks before your due date. Claims can take 4–8 weeks to process. Applying late means lost benefit days you cannot recover.
